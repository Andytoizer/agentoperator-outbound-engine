# Email + LinkedIn structural patterns

Locked rules from real iteration. These are separate from your voice doc
(which is tone / word choice / cadence). These rules govern STRUCTURE and
survive across voices. Don't edit without a reason — they were earned
from 15+ iterations per draft on a real batch.

## Email body — the connector-opener template

Every cold email opens with a peer-customer connector:

> "We work with [peer customer] on their [specific workflow], and since
> [recipient's company] [does the same thing / competes / is adjacent], I'd
> imagine the pattern translates pretty directly."

- **Personalization goes IN the connector**, not after it. Tacked-on personalization ("also I noticed you...") reads like a template with a bolt-on.
- **Customer workflow + real stats.** Verified numbers only. No invented math.
- **Loom or POC offer on its own line.** Never jammed into a paragraph.
- **CTA = open question.** Never a meeting ask in the first email.

## Structural beats (in order)

1. **Greeting.** `Hey [FirstName]-`
2. **Connector.** One sentence, peer customer + your wedge + why it applies to them.
3. **Workflow + stats.** 1-2 sentences with real numbers from the peer customer's usage.
4. **Offer.** Own line: "Happy to share a Loom walking through that workflow if you're interested." or "Happy to run a free POC for your team..."
5. **Research hook.** 1-2 sentences referencing a DATED (<12 months) signal you found during research.
6. **CTA.** Open question: "How's your team handling X today?"
7. **Signoff.** Just your first name.

## Hard voice rules

- Hyphens (`-`), never em dashes (`—`).
- No sentence starts with a colon.
- No listy persona dumps ("superintendents / comms directors / family engagement leads" = salesy).
- No "Apollo" alone — always pair with ZoomInfo ("Apollo/ZoomInfo").
- No invented stats.
- No news signal older than 12 months. Must be dated.
- No prior-job-history hooks ("7 years at X", "came from Y"). Current-company state only.
- Contractions always.
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences).
- Rotate phrasing across a batch — don't reuse "pattern translates pretty directly" four times in one send.

## LinkedIn 1/2 + 2/2 structure

- **1/2** = connect request note. Specific hook + customer-workflow teaser. Target ~200 chars (LinkedIn free-tier limit).
- **2/2** = sent IMMEDIATELY after connect accept. Offers the same Loom / POC as the email. One sentence.

Exception: if the 1/2 already has the CTA baked in ("curious how your team is doing X"), skip 2/2.

## Competitor-adjacent contacts

If the recipient's company competes with or is adjacent to your peer customer, **name it upfront**:

> "Quick heads-up since [Competitor] and [Peer Customer] are clearly competitors — we work with [Peer Customer]..."

Never hide the relationship. Honest disclosure performs better than pretending you didn't notice.

## Subject lines

- Lowercase.
- No colons inside the subject.
- No listy stacks ("3 AE hires + territory build + Q2 pipeline = clean district lists?").
- 4-8 words. Specific to the recipient's situation.
- Examples that work:
  - `the classdojo question`
  - `finding the ~10k non-customer districts`
  - `day-1 lists for the panorama sdr team`
  - `17 years of contractor-data pain`

## A/B test structure (optional)

When running an offer A/B test on the SDR/BDR batch:
- **POC arm** — "send us a sample list, we'll enrich it back for you". Collaborative, lower-barrier.
- **Loom arm** — "happy to share a walkthrough of the workflow". Info-only.

Keep the opener + workflow content IDENTICAL across arms. Only the CTA offer differs, so response-rate delta is attributable to the offer alone.

## What doesn't work (banned from iteration)

- "The next frontier for a team your size..." — unnatural
- "3 new AE hires = clean district lists?" — canned subject pattern
- "The procore parallel" / "the zum district parallel" — prior-job references
- "Scaling an SDR team to a layer deep usually means the biggest pain is..." — fake insight
- Stacked research openers ("$2M/q paid + 200% SQL lift + B2C→B2B background") — feels canned
