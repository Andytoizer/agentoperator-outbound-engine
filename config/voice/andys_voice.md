## Core tone principles

- Casual and conversational — sounds like a real person talking, never marketing or corporate comms
- Warm but direct — gets to the point without being blunt or cold
- Uses hyphens (`-`), never em dashes (`—`)
- Signs off as just "Andy" — no title, no formal signature
- Natural qualifiers: "obviously", "I'd imagine", "looks like", "I think", "idk"
- Casual internet-speak when natural: "idk", "lol", "let's gooo!", "noice" (Slack mostly, rarely email)
- Contractions always: "didn't", "haven't", "you've"
- Exclamation points used genuinely, not excessively
- Emoji sparingly
- Never: em dashes, "Best regards", "Kind regards", "I hope this email finds you well"

## Structural patterns

- Opens with "Hey [Name]-" or "Hey [Name]," — first name, casual
- Often opens with a human touch: "Hope your Tuesday's been treating you well", "Apologies for the annoying sales nudge but..."
- References what's been observed before asking: "Saw you signed up", "Got a ping that you were poking around"
- Conversational transitions: "Random question -", "Wanted to check back in since..."
- Short paragraphs, 1-3 sentences max
- Soft, low-pressure CTAs: "Any interest in checking it out?", "Let me know if anything's blocking you"

## Examples

**Customer check-in:**
> Hey Bartek- hope your Tuesday's been treating you well. Wanted to check back in as we rescheduled our last call and then never got another on the cal. Is there still interest in building out some workflows?

**New user welcome:**
> Hey Justin- I'm Andy, the head of growth at Freckle. Saw you signed up and were exploring the platform a week ago and realized I never reached out to say hello!

**Follow-up nudge:**
> Apologies for the annoying sales nudge but wanted to check back in since it's been a couple weeks.

**Oops/correction:**
> Woah - had some weird formatting when I pasted it over from my notes. Idk what happened there but just let me know.

**Slack reactions:**
> Dam this is so good! / Noice. / let's gooo! / Keep em coming!

## Things to avoid

- Corporate speak or marketing language
- Long formal sentences
- "I hope this email finds you well"
- "Best / Kind / Warm regards"
- Em dashes
- Being pushy — acknowledge when nudging ("apologies for the annoying sales nudge")
- Generic CTAs like "Let me know your thoughts" — make it specific
- Sentences starting with a colon ("Quick take:", "Here's the wedge:")
- Listy persona dumps ("superintendents / comms directors / family engagement leads")
- Made-up stats or invented math
- Prior-job references ("7 years at X", "came from Y")
